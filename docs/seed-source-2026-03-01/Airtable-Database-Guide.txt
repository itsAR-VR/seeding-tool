Kalm AirTable Database Guide
How Your Database Works
This document explains your AirTable database — where all influencer data lives and how to navigate it.
________________


Database Overview
Your database has two main tables:
Table
	Purpose
	People
	Stores all Instagram influencers (approved, pending, and declined)
	Mentions
	Tracks every post and story where influencers mention your brand
	________________


Table 1: People (Instagram Influencers)
This is your main table. It stores every influencer who has been evaluated for your gifting program.
Available Views
Think of views as different "lenses" to look at the same data. Each view filters and shows only what's relevant for that task.
View
	What It Shows
	People
	All influencers (main view)
	Waiting Approval
	Influencers pending your review
	Approved
	Influencers you've approved
	Shopify
	Influencers with product orders
	Mentions
	General brand mentions
	Intervention Flag
	Influencers needing manual attention
	Declined
	Rejected influencers
	________________


View: Waiting Approval
Purpose: Review new influencers and decide whether to approve or decline them.
Fields You'll See
Field
	What It Means
	Username
	Their Instagram handle (@username)
	Approved
	Current status: "Waiting Approval", "Approved", or "Declined"
	Entry Date
	When they were added to the database
	Platform
	Direct link to their Instagram profile
	Full Name
	Their full name
	First Name
	First name (used to personalize emails)
	Email
	Their email address
	Followers
	Total Instagram followers
	Average Views
	Average views per post (shows real engagement)
	Business Address
	Their listed business location (if available)
	Business Category
	Their Instagram category (Digital Creator, Fashion Model, etc.)
	Category
	Content category for your campaigns (Food & Drink, Fashion, Beauty, etc.)
	Sources
	Where you found them
	Notes
	Internal notes about the influencer
	How to Use This View
1. Review each influencer's profile and metrics
2. Check if they match your brand aesthetic and criteria
3. Change Approved to "Approved" or "Declined"
4. Add notes explaining your decision (especially for declines)

View: Approved
Purpose: Manage follow-ups and communication with approved influencers.
Fields You'll See


Field
	What It Means
	Username
	Their Instagram handle
	Approved
	Shows "Approved" for all records in this view
	Full Name
	Their full name
	First Name
	First name for personalized messages
	Email
	Email address for outreach
	First Follow Up Date
	When the first email was sent
	Follow-up Date
	When the last follow-up was sent

	How many times they've responded (0 = no response)

* Identify influencers who need attention

View: Mentions
Purpose: Monitor all organic brand mentions on Instagram — from both influencers and regular users.
Fields You'll See
Field
	What It Means
	Username
	Who mentioned your brand
	Approved
	Whether they're an approved influencer
	Full Name
	Their name (if available)
	Email
	Their email (if known)
	Post
	Number of feed posts mentioning your brand
	History
	Total history of interactions with your brand
	Why This View Matters
* Spot organic brand advocates
* Identify potential new influencers
* Measure word-of-mouth reach
* Distinguish program influencers from regular fans
________________


View: Intervention Flag
Purpose: Identify influencers who need manual attention.
When Influencers Get Flagged
* They haven't responded after multiple follow-ups
* They asked a question the AI couldn't answer
* Something requires human judgment
Fields You'll See
Same as the Approved view — Username, Email, Follow-up counts, Response counts, etc.
How to Use This View
1. Check this view regularly
2. Reach out manually via Instagram DM or personal email
3. Once you get the needed information, enter it in AirTable
4. The automation resumes automatically

View: Mentions (Main View)
Purpose: Track every post and story your influencers create.
Fields You'll See
Field
	What It Means
	Username
	Who created the content
	URL
	Direct link to the content (Instagram post or saved image)
	ContentType
	Type of content: "image" or "video"
	Source
	Where it appeared: "Post" (feed) or "History" (story)
	Notes
	Additional notes about the content
	________________


View: Gallery
Purpose: Visual review of influencer-generated content.
What You'll See
Each mention appears as a visual card showing:
* Username (header)
* Link to the content
* Content type
* Source (post or story)
Why Use This View
* Quick visual scan of all UGC (user-generated content)
* Great for presentations or team reviews
* Easy portfolio review of program results
